- Font: What do you want to communicate?
- Logo Design Categories
-- Lettermark/Monogram Logo: Just text. Uses company name. Reason: When the name is too long, so it's abbreviated. You need to find a way to make a handful of letters iconic, which makes it difficult but possible.
-- Wordmark: Good for startups and local businesses. Gets their name out there. Should be kept short. They show confidence.
-- Brandmark: Simple and easy to recognize globally. No worries about languages. 
-- Combination: Both a logo type and a brandmark. Easiest to work with as a designer.
-- Emblem Logos: Quite niche. An example is Starbucks.
-- Mascot Logos: Also niche. Quite fun. 
- Logo Design Grids: Used in the later stages of the process.
-- Circle and Oval Grids are used for making organic and natural kinds of logos
-- 45 Degree lines, or just lines in general, are really good for very man-made logos
-- Circles can be used to show the width of a part of a design and show how it's maintained throughout the design.
-- Micro White Space
- Logo Warning Signs: 
-- It looks gimmicky, unprofessional, or outdated.
-- Lack of attention to detail. Sloppy or poor execution. Pixelation or Blurriness. Uneven spacing and poor alignment. Inconsistent line thickness. Weird or Complex composition.
-- Never overpromise and under-deliver. Do the opposite.
-- Research and approach design from a problem solving mindset.
--- Target Audience: Who are they? What do they like? What makes them tick? What do they dislike?
--- Psychology: Shape, Color, Typeface
--- Memorability: Memorable Elements, Emotional Connection, Storytelling, Versatility
-- Lack of iteration and revision can greatly harm the logo.
-- Do not rush or mismanage the ending stages of a product (the product packaging and delivery)
- Logo Design Shape Tips
-- Squares and Rectangles are strong and Reliable
-- Ovals and Circles are Community, Friendship, and Love.
-- Angles and Straight lines are linked back to motions and speed.
-- Triangles: Strength and Dependability
-- Experiment with placing your logo design shapes in real life situations to see how they look.
-- Know your client and the target market
-- Marry together word pairs in couples to help get shapes. 
-- Really focus on memorability. Keep It Simple Stupid.
-- Use a Grid.
- Illustrator Shape Building Tool Tips for Logos
-- Gain the Client, Research and Concepts, the use Shape Builder
-- Import Sketches right into Illustrator
-- Change Keyboard Shortcut for Illustrator tool
-- Turn on Gap Recognition
-- De-select Align to Pixel Grid Option
-- Smart Guides: Low Tolerance of 1
-- Selection and Anchor Display: Tolerance of 2. Snap to point 2.
-- Make use of outline mode.
- Offset Path Tool
-- Adds a shape around a shape
- General Design Tips
-- Window -> Actions -> Create Action -> Scale Test -> Record. Scale the logo repeatedly starting at 75%
-- Puppet Warp. Has anchors that anchor the logo in places but allow for some free form edits in others. Use Smooth tool to fix any artifacts.
-- Brands that want to appear man-made must use straight edges and lines.
-- Global Colors

10 Mind Blowing Logo Designing Tips: https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=gza5-1e0IY4
------------------------------------
- White on dark background looks larger than dark on a white background. Offset this with inside stroke.
- Boning Effect: Make the curve of an oval + square shape more gradual.
- The Noun Method: Pick 2 of the best nouns that describe the business. Mash them together into a shape.
- Clearspace around Logo. Find the height. Duplicate around shape in all four directions creating a square on top of that logo. Convert it to an artboard.
- Logo-types: Kerned tighter than they are wide.
- Do not use perfect shapes. Adjust them to be very slightly imperfect to make them look more natural.
- Spend 20 minutes on 20 ideas.
- Reflect your logo types to test them.
- How to know if your logo is alright? Google image reverse search.
- Mind Mapping

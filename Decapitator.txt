Style:
- A: Ultra-Ultra-Smooth. A jaw-dropping amount of gain.
- E: Beefy low end coupled with a smooth but airy top-end sheen.
- N: Weighty but solid low end with focused but not narrow mids. Good for guitar.
- T: Warm and Punchy. Good for adding warmth, dirt, or attitude.
- P: Odd harmonic distortion.

Drive: 
- Harder the input is pushed, the more it is distorted.
- Gain Control.

Punish:
- Adds an additional 20db of gain.

Attitude Meter:
- VU meter paired with Drive. It does not measure the output.

Thump:
- Adds a few db of low frequency boost at the Low Cut Frequency.

Low Cut:
- Remove low frequencies before they hit the saturation circuit.
- Helps to prevent flabbiness.
- At extreme settings, you can get low-fi telephone and AM Radio Effects, especially when paired with the High Cut.

Tone:
- Alters sound before saturation.
- Bright and Dark are the usual EQs that boost one side and lower the other.

High Cut:
- Operates after the saturation.

-----------------------
1. Getting Started: Thumbnails, Content, Ethics
- There are responsibilities. You must understand the ground rules.
- Rules of the thumbnail
-- Relates to the content. Is it misleading?
-- Representation of the Community. How does it represent the community?
-- Respect the Creator's wishes. How does it represent the creator?
2. What should you clip?
- Yes
-- Entertainment, Stories, Informative, Announcements
- No
-- Vulnerable Moments, Sensitive Content, Paywalled Content, Slanderous Content
3. When should you upload?
- Check Streamer Rules
-- Policy for Clip Uploads
- Upload After the Streams
-- Don't upload during stream
-- If you go to the VOD and the chat is rendered, you know that enough time has passed to post your clips.
4. Important Notes
- Do it because you like it, not for validation.
- Just because you clip them, it doesn't mean you're friends.
- Being a clipper/editor doesn't mean you're above other fans.
- Compare yourself to improve, not to put others or yourself down.
- Remember it's borrowed content.
5. Editing Structure
- Clip Workflow
-- Record/Extract the clip
-- Determine the video type
--- Landscape
--- Short Form
-- Create a Thumbnail
-- Catchy, appropriate Title
-- Credit the Streamer
- What kind of clip is it?
-- Informative: Announcement, Educational, Life Updates
-- Story: Story Arc, Challenges, Narrative
-- Entertainment: Funny Moments, Karaoke, Compilations
- Finding Your Style?
-- PNG Animations
-- Sound Effects
-- Memes
-- Cuts and Zooms
-- Subtitles
-- You don't need to be perfect
6. Finding Work
- Build a Portfolio
-- YTjobs / Carrd
-- Set up your rates
-- State your terms of service
-- Make an Editing Reel
-- Put yourself out there
- Networking
-- Social Media, Loopd (Discord Server), Royal Garden (Discord Server)
-- Get to know people 
- Professionalism
-- Be sure to
--- Be polite, Communicate your workflow to the client, Set Deadlines, Follow NDA
--- Be Wary of Taking on too many clients, Unrealistic Expectations, Undervaluing yourself, tying your work to the client.

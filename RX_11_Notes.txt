Spectrogram/Waveform Display
----------------------------
- Louder = brighter
- View -> Spectrogram Settings
-- FTT Size: The greater the resolution. Clearer at larger sizes.
-- Enable Reassignment: Enables a special technique for Spectrogram calculation that allows very precise pitch tracking
for any harmonic components of the signal. 
-- Window: Weighting Functions. Rectangular, may cause vertical blurring. Kaiser or cos3, eliminate leakage at a slight loss of frequency resolution.
-- Frequency Scale:
--- Linear: Most useful when you want to analyze higher frequencies.
--- Logarithmic: More attention on lower frequencies.
--- Mel: Based on how humans perceive sound.
--- Bark: Based on how humans perceive sound by corresponds to a series of frequency bands.
-- Frequency Overlap: Controls the amount of oversampling on the frequency scale. With reassignment, it will increase vertical resolution.
-- Time Overlap:This controls the time oversampling of the Spectrogram. In most cases, overlap of 4x or 8x is a
good setting to start with. However, using higher overlap together with the Reassignment option
will increase the time resolution of a Spectrogram, letting you see transient events clearly.
-- Color Map: Lets you change the color scheme.
-- High-Quality Rendering: Recommended. Makes rendering faster at the cost of some clarity.
-- Reduce Quality Above: Reduces accuracy of preview mode when above x seconds. When you zoom in, it becomes accurate again.
-- Cache-Size: Limits memory usage amount.
- Rulers
-- Right Click Amplitude ruler for scales: db, normalized, 16 bit, percent.
-- Right Click Frequency Ruler:
--- Linear: Hertz are linearly spaced
--- Mel & Bark: Linear below 500hz and logarithmic above it.
--- Log: Different octaves occupy equal screen space.
--- Extended Log: Extends down to 10hz. Attention on low frequencies.
--- Piano Roll: How specific ranges correlate to the western musical scale.
- Waveform displays: Allows you to fade in a waveform over the spectrogram.
- Waveform Overview: Displays the entire audio file.

Repair Assistant
----------------
- Voice/Music mode.
- Click learn to analyze, render to finalize.
- On/Off button allows for a bypass.
- Audition Button lets you hear what is being removed.
- Clean-up:
-- De-noise
-- De-reverb
-- De-hum
-- De-click
- Tone:
-- % controls the amount of boosting and cutting.
-- Tilt: Positive boosts the high end and lowers the low end.
-- Scoop: Positive boosts the high end, boosts the low end, and lowers the mid-range.
-- Tilt/Scoop: Choose which EQ shape.
-- Lo-cut: Toggles low cut filter.
-- Hi-cut: Toggles high cut filter.
- De-Ess
-- Shaping: Determines how much the spectral shape of the sibilant is changed.
-- Threshold: Determines at what level compression begins.
-- Fast/Slow: Attack and release times.
- De-clip:
-- Threshold: Set just below the actual level of clipping.
-- Output Gain: Selects the gain to be applied after,
-- Limiter: Applies a true-peak limited.
- Music Mode is similar.

-----------\
MODULES
-----------\

- Ambience Match
-- Advanced Only
-- Matches the noise floor of one recording to another recording.

- Breath Control
-- Gain Mode: Will reduce the breath by an absolute amount.
-- Target Mode: Sets the level of detected breaths. More natural.
-- Sensitivity: How sensitive the plug-in is.

- Center Extract
-- Advanced Only
-- Preserves either the center or sides of an audio.

- De-Bleed
-- Reduces the leakage of one signal into another.
-- Must have a bleed source track as well.
-- Strength: Determines the amount of bleed reduction.
-- Artifact smoothing: Increase the slider if your output sounds watery, decrease it if the audio sounds dull.

- De-Click
-- Algorithm:
--- Single band: Processes Quickly. Narrow, digital clicks.
--- Multi-Band: Removing regularly repeating clicks with a wider spectrum.
--- Multi-Band (Random Clicks): Wider, Vinyl clicks. Protective algorithm for preserving periodic audio.
--- Low-Latency: Works well on mouth clicks and others.
-- Sensitivity: Increasing too high can impact plosives.
-- Frequency Skew: Negative values are more suitable for generic clicks. Zero or above targets mouth clicks.
-- Click Widening: Extends the repair area around detected clicks, compensating for mouth sounds.

- De-Clip
-- Fixes clipping issues.
-- Often as easy as finding the part you want to fix, and setting the threshold directly underneath it.
-- Histogram Meter: Displays information on the waveform.
-- Quality:
--- High Quality: Slow processing, better results.
--- Start with low quality and move up as needed.
-- Post Limiter: Applies a true peak limiter.

- De-Crackle
-- Many clicks that are close together and low in volume.
-- More effective if it is run after De-Click.
-- Quality: Fast versus quality.
-- Amplitude Skew: If the crackle accompanies transients or high level signal passages, set this more to the right. If it's low-amplitude instead, set this more to the left.

- De-ess
-- Classic Mode: Broadband Gain Envelope.
-- Spectral Mode: Multiband compressor.
-- Threshold:
--- Relative Mode: Determines the level of speech and sets it relative to that.
--- Absolute Mode: Sets the threshold to a decibel level below full scale.
-- Cutoff Frequency: The lower boundary for sibilance detection.
-- Spectral Flattening: How much the shape of the sibilant is changed (flattened). 0% leaves natural shape.
-- Spectral Tilt: Brown noise gives it a darker sound, white noise a brighter one. 0 creates a natural spectral decay close to pink noise. Values below 0 give brown, above give white.
-- Speed:
--- Fast: Quick Attack and Release.
--- Slow: Longer attack and release times.

- De-Hum
-- Removes persistent tonal noises, like ACs.
-- Static: Ideal for simple tonal noises. Hum doesn't overlap speech frequencies.
-- Dynamic: For the more complex ones.
-- Controls in Dynamic Mode:
--- Learn: Isolate hum if possible.
--- Adaptive Mode: Better if hum changes in pitch.
--- Bands: Adjust to higher value for complex hums, but the tradeoff is transparency.
--- Filter Q: Adjust the bandwidth of the notch filters.
--- Range Selectors: Sets the action region of the hum removal.
-- Controls in Static Mode:
--- Base Frequency: Adjust the slider until you find the point where the hum is removed.
--- Frequency: Sets the fundamental frequency of the filter.
--- Filter Q: Controls the bandwidth of the notch filters.
--- Linear Phase Filters: The filters provide accurate frequency response with no change in phase at the expense of latency and filter pre-ringing.
--- Harmonics: Can add up to 16.
--- Link Harmonics: Different gain selections for the harmonics.
--- Slope: Sets how aggressively the harmonics are attenuated.
--- Filter DC Offset: A filter to remove and DC offset.
-- Should be used prior to Voice or Spectral.
-- Hums appear like solid orange lines.

- De-Plosive:
-- Use it before applying any high-pass filter!
-- Sensitivity: 
--- Lower values narrow it down, but preserve quality.
-- Strength:
--- To maintain speech signal, try upping sensitivity while reducing strength.
-- Frequency Limits:
--- Plosives are typically 20 to 300, but may sometimes go as high as 500. Keep this as low as you can.
-- Plosives look like strong vertical lines that appear brighter than the signal around them.

Spectrogram/Waveform Display
----------------------------
- Louder = brighter
- View -> Spectrogram Settings
-- FTT Size: The greater the resolution. Clearer at larger sizes.
-- Enable Reassignment: Enables a special technique for Spectrogram calculation that allows very precise pitch tracking
for any harmonic components of the signal. 
-- Window: Weighting Functions. Rectangular, may cause vertical blurring. Kaiser or cos3, eliminate leakage at a slight loss of frequency resolution.
-- Frequency Scale:
--- Linear: Most useful when you want to analyze higher frequencies.
--- Logarithmic: More attention on lower frequencies.
--- Mel: Based on how humans perceive sound.
--- Bark: Based on how humans perceive sound by corresponds to a series of frequency bands.
-- Frequency Overlap: Controls the amount of oversampling on the frequency scale. With reassignment, it will increase vertical resolution.
-- Time Overlap:This controls the time oversampling of the Spectrogram. In most cases, overlap of 4x or 8x is a
good setting to start with. However, using higher overlap together with the Reassignment option
will increase the time resolution of a Spectrogram, letting you see transient events clearly.
-- Color Map: Lets you change the color scheme.
-- High-Quality Rendering: Recommended. Makes rendering faster at the cost of some clarity.
-- Reduce Quality Above: Reduces accuracy of preview mode when above x seconds. When you zoom in, it becomes accurate again.
-- Cache-Size: Limits memory usage amount.
- Rulers
-- Right Click Amplitude ruler for scales: db, normalized, 16 bit, percent.
-- Right Click Frequency Ruler:
--- Linear: Hertz are linearly spaced
--- Mel & Bark: Linear below 500hz and logarithmic above it.
--- Log: Different octaves occupy equal screen space.
--- Extended Log: Extends down to 10hz. Attention on low frequencies.
--- Piano Roll: How specific ranges correlate to the western musical scale.
- Waveform displays: Allows you to fade in a waveform over the spectrogram.
- Waveform Overview: Displays the entire audio file.

-----------\
MODULES
-----------\

- Ambience Match
-- Advanced Only
-- Matches the noise floor of one recording to another recording.

- Breath Control
-- Gain Mode: Will reduce the breath by an absolute amount.
-- Target Mode: Sets the level of detected breaths. More natural.
-- Sensitivity: How sensitive the plug-in is.

- Center Extract
-- Advanced Only
-- Preserves either the center or sides of an audio.

- De-Bleed
-- Reduces the leakage of one signal into another.
-- Must have a bleed source track as well.
-- Strength: Determines the amount of bleed reduction.
-- Artifact smoothing: Increase the slider if your output sounds watery, decrease it if the audio sounds dull.

- De-Click
-- Algorithm:
--- Single band: Processes Quickly. Narrow, digital clicks.
--- Multi-Band: Removing regularly repeating clicks with a wider spectrum.
--- Multi-Band (Random Clicks): Wider, Vinyl clicks. Protective algorithm for preserving periodic audio.
--- Low-Latency: Works well on mouth clicks and others.
-- Sensitivity: Increasing too high can impact plosives.
-- Frequency Skew: Negative values are more suitable for generic clicks. Zero or above targets mouth clicks.
-- Click Widening: Extends the repair area around detected clicks, compensating for mouth sounds.

- De-Clip
-- Fixes clipping issues.
-- Often as easy as finding the part you want to fix, and setting the threshold directly underneath it.
-- Histogram Meter: Displays information on the waveform.
-- Quality:
--- High Quality: Slow processing, better results.
--- Start with low quality and move up as needed.
-- Post Limiter: Applies a true peak limiter.

- De-Crackle
-- Many clicks that are close together and low in volume.
-- More effective if it is run after De-Click.
-- Quality: Fast versus quality.
-- Amplitude Skew: If the crackle accompanies transients or high level signal passages, set this more to the right. If it's low-amplitude instead, set this more to the left.

- De-ess
-- Classic Mode: Broadband Gain Envelope.
-- Spectral Mode: Multiband compressor.
-- Threshold:
--- Relative Mode: Determines the level of speech and sets it relative to that.
--- Absolute Mode: Sets the threshold to a decibel level below full scale.
-- Cutoff Frequency: The lower boundary for sibilance detection.
-- Spectral Flattening: How much the shape of the sibilant is changed (flattened). 0% leaves natural shape.
-- Spectral Tilt: Brown noise gives it a darker sound, white noise a brighter one. 0 creates a natural spectral decay close to pink noise. Values below 0 give brown, above give white.
-- Speed:
--- Fast: Quick Attack and Release.
--- Slow: Longer attack and release times.

- De-Hum
-- Removes persistent tonal noises, like ACs.
-- Static: Ideal for simple tonal noises. Hum doesn't overlap speech frequencies.
-- Dynamic: For the more complex ones.
-- Controls in Dynamic Mode:
--- Learn: Isolate hum if possible.
--- Adaptive Mode: Better if hum changes in pitch.
--- Bands: Adjust to higher value for complex hums, but the tradeoff is transparency.
--- Filter Q: Adjust the bandwidth of the notch filters.
--- Range Selectors: Sets the action region of the hum removal.
-- Controls in Static Mode:
--- Base Frequency: Adjust the slider until you find the point where the hum is removed.
--- Frequency: Sets the fundamental frequency of the filter.
--- Filter Q: Controls the bandwidth of the notch filters.
--- Linear Phase Filters: The filters provide accurate frequency response with no change in phase at the expense of latency and filter pre-ringing.
--- Harmonics: Can add up to 16.
--- Link Harmonics: Different gain selections for the harmonics.
--- Slope: Sets how aggressively the harmonics are attenuated.
--- Filter DC Offset: A filter to remove and DC offset.
-- Should be used prior to Voice or Spectral.
-- Hums appear like solid orange lines.

- De-Plosive:
-- Use it before applying any high-pass filter!
-- Sensitivity: 
--- Lower values narrow it down, but preserve quality.
-- Strength:
--- To maintain speech signal, try upping sensitivity while reducing strength.
-- Frequency Limits:
--- Plosives are typically 20 to 300, but may sometimes go as high as 500. Keep this as low as you can.
-- Plosives look like strong vertical lines that appear brighter than the signal around them.

- De-Reverb:
-- Reverb Profile: Bands. Can also be used to adjust ringing or muddiness.
-- Tail-Length: Controls decay of De-Reverb processing. Increase if reverb tails reappear after processing.
--- Decrease if it sounds overprocessed or dull.
-- Artifact Smoothing: May need to balance this and reduction. Decreasing it makes it more accurate, instead of smooth.
-- Enhance Dry Signal: Help create more dynamic range. A good option to try when working with voice or other transient heavy material.
-- On the Spectrogram, reverberant audio looks more "blended together." This means the horizontal view of the transients doesn't clearly look separated into lines.
-- Tips for Dealing with complicated Reverbs: Multiple passes
--- Train the De-Reverb and set the reduction amount to a value that yields good results on the long tail.
--- Learn a new Reverb Profile and try reducing the level of early reflections: set the tail length control to 0.5, Artifact smoothing around 3.0, increase reduction.
--- A combination of De-Reverb and Spectral De-noise can help.

- De-Rustle:
-- Advanced Only
-- Reduce noise of cloth brushing the microphone.

- De-Wind:
-- Advanced Only
-- Remove low frequency rumble from wind.

- Deconstruct
-- Advanced Only
-- Analyzes audio and separates the signal into Tonal, Noisy, and Transient components.

- Dialogue Contour
-- Advanced Only
-- Manipulate the pitch envelope.

- Dialogue Isolate
-- Particularly useful for increasing the level of the dialogue in challenging low signal-to-noise ratio recordings.
-- Sensitivity: 
--- Higher Values: Broader range but can introduce artifacts.
--- Lower Values: Can result in more noise being included but it maintains clarity.
-- For Stationary Noise, if the results aren't good try Spectral De-Noise.

- Guitar De-Noise
-- Amp section handles hum or buzz from guitar pickups or amplifiers.
--- Must be tonal or static. If not, address the broadband issues with Spectral or Voice De-noise first.
--- Sensitivity: Higher sensitivity when the amp of noise varies over time.
--- Resolution: Default is usually enough, but higher can be used.
-- Squeak for controlling fret and finger squeaks.
--- Duration: Short is good for the majority of squeaks.
--- Long: Can handles short and long ones. Has higher latency.
-- Pick for reducing the attack of picker or plucked guitar strings if they stand out too much.
--- Sensitivity: Higher will affect the tone.
-- Amp does not work well on broadband noises.
-- Squeak may need manual correction. Run it on higher sensitivity in smaller, isolated sections. Spectral Repair also helps.
-- If Pick isn't enough, De-ess in Spectral mode or De-click are good options.

- Interpolate
-- Repairing clicks below 4000 samples.

- Loudness Optimize
-- A tool for analyzing loudness.
-- Anything below the threshold does not contribute.
-- Measured %: How much of the audio in the file is being measured.
-- Integrated LUFS: Displays the integrated loudness.
-- Boost: Max amount of gain applied to quiet sections.
-- Threshold: The level at which upward compression will start to be applied.
-- Ratio: Amount of compression
-- Speed: How quickly the processor will begin to boost when the audio falls below the threshold.

- Mouth De-Click
-- Increasing sensitivity can impact plosives.
-- Zero or above targets mouth, negative vinyl.
-- Extends the area around detected clicks, compensating for mouth sounds.
-- Running it twice can usually offer better results, since louder clicks can mask quieter ones.

- Music Rebalance
-- Breaks apart different elements of a song and allows you to adjust them.
-- Adjusts the attention of the stem splitting algorithm to a specific stem.

- Spectral De-noise
-- Designed to remove stationary or slowly changing tonal noise.
-- Threshold: Higher reduces noise but can suppress low-level signal components. Lower may result in noise modulation. If background noise changes over time, raise the threshold.
-- Reduction: Only apply as much as needed.
-- Quality: Affects CPU usage. A is the lowers, D the highest.
-- Artifact Control: Lower values can lead to chirpy or watery sounds. Higher values can result in bursts of noise right after the signal falls below the threshold.
-- Smoothing: Allows for sharper of mor graduals slopes between edit curve points. Controls interpolation amount.
-- Reduction Curve: Allows for fine tuning of the reduction curve. Higher edit points result in less noise reduction in the region, lower edit points result in more.

- Spectral Repair
-- Intelligently removes undesired sounds.
-- Attenuate Mode: Attenuate reduces spectrogram magnitudes in the selected area to match magnitudes from the surrounding area, resulting in the removal of the sound without leaving an audible gap behind.
-- Bands: Higher number has better resolution, but requires more analysis. Lower is ideal for processing short selections or transients.
-- Surrounding Region Length: How much of the surrounding content will be used for interpolation.
-- Strength: Strength of attenuation.
-- Multi-Resolution: Better frequency and time resolutions.
-- Before/After Weighting: Gives more weight to the surrounding audio before or after the selection.
-- Direction of the Interpolation: Horizontal, Vertical, 2D (all directions)
-- Replace Mode: Can be used to replace badly damaged sections in tonal audio with surrounding data.
-- Pattern Mode: Pattern mode is suitable for badly damaged audio with background noise or for audio with repeating parts
-- Partials and Noise: The advanced version of Replace mode. It restores harmonics of the audio more accurately with control over the Harmonic sensitivity parameter. Partials + Noise is able to correctly interpolate cases of pitch modulation, including vibrato.
-- Harmonic Sensitivity: Lower values will detect fewer. Higher values will detect more but can introduce unnatural pitch modulations.
-- Use directly on the spectrogram.
-- When used with Time Selection, it can provide higher quality processing than de-click for long corrupted segments.
-- Find Similar Event Tool can be used to help repair multiple small sections instead of repairing it all at once.

- Spectral Recovery
-- Advanced Only
-- Adds missing frequencies to bandwidth-limited speech content and patches holes in the frequency spectrum due to compression artifacts.

- Streaming Preview
-- Applies the same loudness normalization that streaming platforms will apply to preview it.

- Voice De-noise
-- Filter Type: Surgical: Removes more, but may artifact with watery or chirpy noises. Gentle: More transparent, but removes less high end "sizzle"

- Wow & Flutter
-- Advanced Only
-- Transparent correction of pitch variations due to inconsistent speed of the recording medium.

- Azimuth: The Azimuth module provides control over left and right channel gain and delay. 

- Dither
-- A process when converting a higher bit resolution to a lower one.
-- Noise Shaping: Higher noise shaping can cause slightly higher peaks in your signal.
-- Dither Amount: Normal is usually good. No/Lower dithering can leave distortion or modulation, too high will eliminate it but increase the noise floor. High with no noise shaping can create white noise.
-- Auto-Blanking: Automatically mutes dither output.
-- Limit Noise Peaks: If high peaks are undesirable, enable to suppress them.
-- Suppress Harmonics: If any noise is undesirable, suppress harmonics. This moves any harmonic quantization distortion away from overtones.

- EQ Match
-- Advanced Only
-- Lets you match the EQ profile of a selection with the profile of a different selection.

- Fade
-- Provide gradual increase or decrease in gain throughout the selection.
-- Cosine is good for in-phase tonal material.
-- Equal power for noisy material.

- Leveler
-- Advanced Only
-- Rides that gain in your file to even out the variations of the signal level.

- Loudness Control
-- The Loudness Control module adjusts the gain of the signal in order to meet the specified loudness standard
-- Integrated Loudness: The desired target.
-- Tolerance: Margin of error, typically set to 0.5-2.
-- Short-term/Momentary Loudness: Short term over 3 seconds, momentary is 400ms.
-- True Peak: True peak value of the clip.
-- Program Loudness Gate: The cutoff of low level signals to exclude it from the loudness computation.

- Mixing
-- Provides control over both left and right signal and their balance levels. Can be used to downmix stereo into mono, invert waveforms, transcode left/right to mid/side, subtract a center channel, and more.
-- Left Output Mix: Define how much of the current selection's left and right channel signal will be present in the new target left channel.
-- Right Output Mix: Define how much of the current selection's left and right channel signal will be present in the new target right channel.

- Normalize
-- Sets sample peak level to the specified target peak.

- Phase
-- Rotates signal phase to balance asymmetrical waveforms.
-- Spreads peaks more evenly.

- Resample
-- Convert an audio file from one sampling rate to another.
-- Change Tag Only: Only changes the file properties. Effectively altering the playback rate and pitch.
-- Pre-Ringing: Intermediate values between 0 and 1 produce so-called intermediate-phase filters that balance pre- and post-ringing while maintaining linear-phase response across a possibly wider range of frequencies.

- Signal Generator
-- Synthesize silence, tones, and noise.

- Time & Pitch
-- Independent control over the length and pitch of audio. It is useful for retuning audio to fit in a mix better.
-- Algorithm:
--- Radius: Highest Quality.
--- Radius RT: Faster than Radius.
--- Solo Instrument: Good for monophonic. Adaptive window too small, there will be a squeaking noise. Too big, it will become grainy.
-- Formant Correction: Resonant Frequencies that tend to be perceived as characteristics like age and gender. Strength is usually 1, shift is usually 0. If you hear what sounds like an EQ adjustment, lower strength.
--- For special effects, like changing perceived gender, try adjusting the semitones.
-- Stretch Ratio: 12.5% to 100% will cause the audio to speed up without affecting pitch.
--- 100% to 800% will cause the audio to slow down without affecting pitch.
-- BPM Calculator
-- Pitch Shift: How much will be applied
-- Transient Sensitivity: Determines the algorithm’s handling of transient material. Higher values will result in better preservation of individual transients after processing.
-- Noise Generation: Helps noisy material sound more natural.
-- Pitch Coherence: Helps preserve the natural timbre for pitched solo voices. High values of pitch coherence will avoid phasiness in Radius’s output at the expense of roughness (modulation) in processed polyphonic recordings. 
-- Phase Coherence: Preserves the coherence of phase between the left and right channels.

- Variable Pitch
-- Can be used to quickly fix small pitch variations or correct gradual pitch drifts.
-- Contour Curve. Nodes can be added to the curve and adjusted to make changes.
-- Ctrl+Click to remove a node.
-- Preserve Time: Radius used when enabled.
--- Pitch Coherence
--- Transient Sensitivity

- Variable Time
-- Correct timing issues or creatively adjust speed.
-- Controls same as Variable

- Find Similar
-- Similarity: Scale of how similar they are

- Markers and Regions
-- M Key adds marker or region at playhead.

- Spectrum Analyzer

- Waveform Statistics
-- Shows info about waveform in the current selection

- Identifying Problems: Use Graph in Docs

- Using the Spectrogram Display
-- Hum is shown as a series of horizontal lines. Usually with a bright one at 50hz or 60hz.
-- Buzz is similar to hum but at a higher frequency.
-- Hiss and other Broadband noise: Speckles that surround the program material.
-- Clicks, pops, & Short Impulse Noise: Vertical lines. The louder they are, the brighter they are.
-- Clipping: Makes waveform appear squared off.
-- Intermittent Noises: Harder to spot, easier to hear.
-- Gaps and Drop Outs: Delete the gap and apply spectral repair.
